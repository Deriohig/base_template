The Digital Hub

The Digital Hub is an interactive and engaging bespoke project. The website was developed in order to promote the brands identity as a vibrant, innovation focused enterprise while also providing its user base information in a readable yet stylish manner.

The Digital Hub

Working on the Digital Hub

The Digital Hub was a challenging project to work on, mainly due to my unfamiliarity with wordpress at the time and the complexities of the layout. The Hub was an 8-column grid layout, based around square principles and Material Design, meaning that most everything was a square and everything had a place on the z-index (think shadows and a third dimension).

Despite our best efforts to maintain the square, we had to drop it as the content didnt always fit our beloved squares. Despite this, the website was an eye opener into what was possible in brochure sites. Strong typography, clever design, bold but stylish use of contrast and colour, the Digital Hub tried hard to inovate.

In my opinion it did it with great sucess given my knowledge of the budget, and dedication of the team.

Challenges

The squares

The design required that most every box was a perfect square. This was fine at the begining when we were developing for desktop with placeholder content, but as development went on it became clear that the squares were breaking on certain screen sizes when content overflowed or looked off. Losing time for the remaining development, we decided to drop the square in some cases.

Masonary grid

The masonary grid posed challenges in communication and execution. The masonary grid was implemented three times in order to get the correct layout that was dynamic, responsive, and easy to use. The problem we faced was that there was no initial spec, meaning that the initial understanding we had was misinterpreted. The final sollution was to have both front and backend developers sit down and complete it together.

Budget

Unfortunately the project went overbudget. The unforseen aspects over the course of the project werent accounted for in the estimates, and even if there were no unforscene circumstances I doubt it would have been done in time. This project taught us all the importance of tracking time for implementation, that way when we encountered the same sort of work we would have a better basis for a correct estimate.

Credit Union Framework

The template enabled us to rapidly create a website for the numerous Credit Union's across Ireland giving them everything they needed to provide a better, more efficient service to their members.
