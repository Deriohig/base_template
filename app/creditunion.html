The Credit Union Framework

The Credit Union Framework is a reusable component based framework that allows for rapid website development and is being used by a number of credit unions. As the main full stack developer on the framework I was resonsible for the majority of the frameworks development. This included individual site content upload, support, and deployments.

Working on the Credit Union Framework

The Credit Union framework was a project where I was in charge of full-stack development, live and staging deployments, content management, and conversing with the client over the best way to implement their needs.

The framework is a long running project that will continue to be used to develop Credit Unions across Ireland into the forseeable future. It provides a cost effective way for the Credit Unions to band together, keep ahead of the curve, and provide engagement in a younger audience (in consideration of their issues with balancing demographics).

The method of implementation, using components as building blocks, is being heavily used by the company into the future. This method minimises future development time by being able to pull from a range of pre-built, pre-tested, and pre-configured components. The remaining development time can then then be used to focus on other bespoke functionality, leading to a higher quality of work and less pressure to piece together large sites in short timeframes.

Challenges

Responsibility

The Credit Union framework was the first project I was given sole responsibility over the codebase. This was invaluable as I got to demonstrate my improvement in skill - both soft and hard - which I had built during my time in the company.

Deployments

Finding a bug in the codebase was the most painful issue with the framework. A single bug needed to be fixed in five different git repositories, which made it more time consuming and monotonous. For security reasons all the code bases needed to be seperated. Minimising bugs was important to avoid this, although edge cases are forever present.

The Calculator

The calculator provided challenges in both responsive layout and providing the information the client needed. The final product was a more fleshed out version of the initial design, giving a more detailed breakdown of the loan and relative information. I kept as close to the initial design as possible, and tweaked it here and there until the client was happy. I enjoyed implementing this piece of functionality a lot, despite the challenges.
